## FCC COMPLIANCE RULES — READ FIRST
*Terrence reviews every script. Bishop Hank audits quarterly.*

**ALLOWED in underwriting acknowledgments:**
- Business name and location
- Brief neutral description of products or services
- Website URL, phone number (as identification only)
- Value-neutral descriptions ("a family-owned business serving Northeast Ohio since 1987")

**NEVER ALLOWED:**
- Calls to action ("Call today," "Visit us," "Stop by")
- Comparative or qualitative language ("the best," "finest," "unmatched")
- Price mentions or promotional offers ("50% off," "free estimates")
- Inducements to purchase ("you'll love it," "don't miss out")
- Anything that sounds like a commercial advertisement

**When in doubt, leave it out. A plain, factual acknowledgment is always safe.**

### OBJECTION HANDLING

**"We already advertise on [other station]."**
"That's great — and we're not asking you to replace that. WNZN Power reaches a specific audience that other stations don't touch the same way. These are gospel music listeners — they're loyal, they're faith-driven, and they pay attention to who supports their community. It's a different relationship than advertising."

**"Is this tax-deductible?"**
"WNZN Power is operated by P.A.C.E. Foundation, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Underwriting contributions may be deductible as a business expense or charitable contribution depending on how your accountant classifies it. I'd encourage you to consult your tax advisor — I just want to make sure you have the right information."

**"What's the difference between underwriting and advertising?"**
"Great question. Traditional advertising is a commercial — it's promotional, it has calls to action, it tries to sell something. Underwriting is different. It's factual recognition — your name, what you do, and how to find you. No hype. No pressure. For a noncommercial station like ours, that's actually required by the FCC. A lot of business owners find it more dignified than a typical ad."

**"I need to think about it."**
"Of course. Can I ask — is there anything specific you'd want to know more about before making a decision? I want to make sure you have everything you need. And if it's a budget timing question, we can talk about when in the year makes sense to start."

**"We don't have budget right now."**
"I understand. When does your budget cycle reset? I'd love to stay in touch and reconnect when the timing is better. In the meantime, can I put you on our underwriter list so you get our quarterly updates? A lot of businesses join after they've been on our radar for a while."
