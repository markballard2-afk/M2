## WHAT THE GOOGLE AD GRANT IS

Google gives qualifying 501(c)(3) nonprofits **$10,000 per month** in free Google Search advertising — that's $120,000/year in ad spend at no cost. Ads appear on Google Search when people search for terms related to your mission.

**What it does for WNZN Power:**
- Drives new listeners to WNZN.org
- Surfaces WNZN Power when people search for gospel radio in Northeast Ohio
- Promotes Spring Gospel Jam and events to people actively searching
- Grows the donor pipeline by capturing people already interested in gospel music and community media
- Supports Google Ad Grant compliance by maintaining an active, quality website

**What it does NOT do:** It is not cash. It cannot pay bills. It funds Google Search ads only.

## ONGOING COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
*Google will suspend the Ad Grant if these are not maintained.*

### Monthly Requirements (Jamie checks, Seven reports)
- [ ] **Click-through rate (CTR) above 5%** — If any campaign falls below 5% for two months, Google suspends the grant. Jamie pauses low-performing keywords immediately.
- [ ] **No single-word keywords** — Google prohibits broad single-word keywords. Always use phrases of 2+ words.
- [ ] **No overly generic keywords** — "Radio," "music," "gospel" alone are not allowed. Must be specific.
- [ ] **Active account** — Must make at least one change to the account monthly (new ad, keyword update, bid adjustment).
- [ ] **Quality Score** — Ads must be relevant to landing pages. Tom Jones keeps landing pages updated.
- [ ] **Conversion tracking active** — GA4 must be connected and recording conversions.

### Quarterly Requirements
- [ ] Jamie submits a brief impact report to Clarence "Papa" Ballard: ad spend used, clicks, conversions, highlights.
- [ ] Seven Townsel reviews GA4 data for campaign performance trends.
- [ ] Tom Jones confirms website health and landing page quality.

### Annual
- [ ] Renew Google for Nonprofits verification (Percent/TechSoup check required annually).
