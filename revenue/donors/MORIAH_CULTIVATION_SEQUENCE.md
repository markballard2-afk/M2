# WNZN POWER / P.A.C.E. FOUNDATION — MAJOR DONOR CULTIVATION SEQUENCE
*Agent Owner: Moriah Ballard | Closer: Louise | Personal Closer: Dr. Mark Ballard Sr.*

---

## THE PHILOSOPHY

Major donors don't give to organizations. They give to people they trust, causes they believe in, and leaders who inspire them. Dr. Ballard is the reason people give to WNZN Power. Moriah's job is to build the relationship, prepare the ask, and make it easy for Dr. Ballard to be the one who seals it.

No ask before the relationship. No relationship without a plan. No gift without a thank-you the same day.

---

## PROSPECT QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

Before anyone enters the cultivation sequence, Moriah answers these four questions:

1. **Capacity** — Can they give $5,000 or more? (Look at profession, business ownership, home, church giving history)
2. **Connection** — Do they already know and respect Dr. Ballard or WNZN Power?
3. **Cause alignment** — Do they believe in gospel music, community media, or the Black church tradition?
4. **Availability** — Are they reachable? Would they take a call from Dr. Ballard?

If yes to 3 of 4, they go on the prospect list. If yes to all 4, they go to the top 10.

## THE SIX-TOUCH CULTIVATION SEQUENCE
*From first contact to the ask. Minimum 8 weeks. Maximum 6 months.*

---

### TOUCH 1: THE DISCOVERY CALL
**Who:** Dr. Mark Ballard Sr. personally  
**When:** Week 1  
**Goal:** Start the relationship. Learn what they care about. Do NOT ask for money.

**Script for Dr. Ballard:**

"[Name], this is Mark Ballard — Dr. Mark Ballard from WNZN Power here in Lorain. How are you doing?

I'm calling because [how you know them / who referred you / how you heard of them]. I've been thinking about the people in this community who really understand what we're trying to do with gospel radio, and your name kept coming up.

I'm not calling to ask you for anything today. I just wanted to introduce myself properly and learn a little more about you and what you're passionate about. What is it that keeps you connected to this community?"

*Let them talk. Listen more than you speak. Ask follow-up questions. Find the emotional connection — faith, family, legacy, community, music. Take notes for Moriah.*

**End of call:**
"I'd love to get together sometime — maybe a cup of coffee or a tour of the station. Would you be open to that?"

*Moriah follows up with a handwritten note from Dr. Ballard within 48 hours.*

---

### TOUCH 2: THE STATION TOUR OR COFFEE MEETING
**Who:** Dr. Ballard (or Moriah if Dr. Ballard unavailable)  
**When:** Week 2–4  
**Goal:** Let them feel the mission. Make it real. Still no ask.

**Station Tour Talking Points:**
- "This is where the magic happens every morning. These are the voices that people in Lorain wake up to."
- "We've been broadcasting gospel music since [year]. There are people who tell me WNZN Power got them through the hardest seasons of their lives."
- "Right now we reach [X] listeners across Northeast Ohio. But the vision is to go far beyond that — through our streaming platform, PGN, we can reach gospel music listeners anywhere."
- "The thing I want you to understand is that we're not just a radio station. We're a community institution. When Lorain goes through something — a loss, a celebration, a crisis — WNZN Power is the sound of home."

**Coffee Meeting Alternative:**
Same conversation, without the tour. Bring a one-page impact document (see below). Share 2–3 listener stories that illustrate what WNZN Power means to people.

*After the meeting: Moriah sends a personal email thanking them for their time. Attach the impact document if not given in person.*

---

### TOUCH 5: THE PERSONAL NOTE
**Who:** Dr. Mark Ballard Sr., handwritten  
**When:** After Touch 4 event, or at the 6-week mark if no event available  
**Goal:** Human connection. Nobody writes handwritten notes anymore. This one lands.

**Note Template:**

"[Name] —

Thank you for spending time with us. I don't take it lightly when someone gives their attention to what we're building.

What we're doing at WNZN Power matters — and I believe you know that. I look forward to continuing our conversation.

God bless you,
Mark Ballard"

*Short. Personal. In his own handwriting.*

---

### TOUCH 6: THE ASK
**Who:** Moriah sets it up. Dr. Ballard makes the ask in person or by phone. Louise follows up.  
**When:** After 5 meaningful touches. Not before.  
**Goal:** Secure the first gift.

**The Ask Conversation (Dr. Ballard):**

"[Name], I want to be straight with you because I respect you too much to beat around the bush.

I'm building something in Lorain that I believe in with everything I have. WNZN Power has been here for [X] years, and I want it here for the next 50. We're in a moment right now where the right investment can take us from where we are to where we need to be.

I'm asking you to consider making a gift of [$10,000 / $15,000 / $25,000 — pick one specific number] to support WNZN Power and the P.A.C.E. Foundation this year. That gift goes directly toward [specific use: programming, technology upgrade, PGN expansion — be specific].

I'm not going to pressure you. But I want you to know that I wouldn't be having this conversation with you if I didn't believe in you — and if I didn't believe you believe in what we're doing.

What are your thoughts?"

*Then be quiet. Let them respond. Don't fill the silence.*

## POST-GIFT STEWARDSHIP SEQUENCE
*The gift is the beginning, not the end.*

### Same Day as Gift
- Dr. Ballard calls personally. Not a voicemail — a real conversation. Two minutes. "I wanted you to hear my voice. Thank you."

### Within 48 Hours
- **Official acknowledgment letter** mailed (Moriah sends). Must include:
  - Date of gift
  - Amount
  - Statement that no goods or services were provided in exchange (required for IRS)
  - P.A.C.E. Foundation EIN
  - Dr. Ballard's signature

**Acknowledgment Letter Template:**

---

Dear [Name],

On behalf of P.A.C.E. Foundation and WNZN Power 89.1 FM, I want to personally thank you for your generous gift of **$[amount]** received on **[date]**.

Your investment in this mission matters more than I can express in a letter. Know that every program we air, every listener we reach, and every artist we uplift carries a piece of your generosity with it.

Please retain this letter for your tax records. P.A.C.E. Foundation is a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (EIN: [number]). No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution.

With deep gratitude,

Dr. Mark A. Ballard, Sr.
P.A.C.E. Foundation | WNZN Power 89.1 FM

---

### 30 Days After Gift
- Moriah sends a personal email update: "I wanted to share how your gift is already making a difference…" (Be specific — mention something real that happened.)

### 90 Days After Gift
- **Impact Update** — One page. What has WNZN Power done in the last 90 days? Listeners reached, events held, artists featured, community moments. Make the donor feel like an insider.

### Invite to Next Event
- VIP invitation to next WNZN Power event. Same treatment as Touch 4 but now they're family, not a prospect.

### End of Year (November–December)
- Moriah sends year-end giving letter. Remind them of their gift earlier in the year. Share the year's highlights. Make a soft second ask or upgrade ask.

**Year-End Giving Letter Template:**

---

Dear [Name],

As this year comes to a close, I find myself thinking about the people who made it possible.

You are one of those people.

Your gift of $[amount] earlier this year helped WNZN Power 89.1 FM [specific accomplishment]. That is your impact. That is your legacy in Lorain, Ohio.

As we head into a new year, I'm asking you to consider renewing your support — or, if you're in a position to do so, increasing your gift to **$[upgraded amount]**. 

You can give online at [website], mail a check to P.A.C.E. Foundation at [address], or call Moriah at [phone] and she'll take care of everything.

However you give, know that you are not just donating to a radio station. You are investing in a community, a calling, and a future.

Thank you for being in our corner.

With faith and gratitude,
Dr. Mark A. Ballard, Sr.
